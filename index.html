ABOUT MODERNAS

Welcome to our website dedicated to exploring and celebrating the rich literary heritage of Spanish women writers from early modernity. Our mission is to bring to light the often-overlooked contributions of these remarkable women, including María de Zayas y Sotomayor, María Egual, Leonor de Meneses Noronha, Mariana de Carvajal y Saavedra, and Ana Francisca Abarca de Bolea y Mur. These writers defied the gender roles of their time and broke through the barriers that held them back, leaving behind a legacy of powerful, insightful, and influential literary works that continue to inspire and captivate readers today. On this website, you’ll find in-depth research, insightful analysis, and fascinating historical context about the lives and works of these trailblazing writers. Our team of dedicated researchers is committed to uncovering the hidden gems of Spanish women’s literature, and we invite you to join us on this journey of discovery. We hope this website serves as a valuable resource for scholars, students, and enthusiasts alike and sparks a renewed appreciation for the groundbreaking contributions of Spanish women writers throughout history. Thank you for visiting, and we invite you to explore the fascinating world of early modern Spanish women’s literature with us.

KEY FEATURES OF OUR WEBSITE

Catalogue

Discover our comprehensive catalogue showcasing the influential female contributors to 17th-century Spanish novela corta. Explore annotated bibliographies highlighting their key works and recurring themes. Immerse yourself in primary sources, including manuscripts and early editions, alongside secondary sources offering valuable context and analysis. This catalogue consolidates the works of our authors, enabling a nuanced understanding of their invaluable contributions to Early Modern Spain's literary landscape.

Visualization Tools

Explore our Visualization Tools page for an interactive journey into these texts. Gain a fresh perspective on their literary works using cutting-edge tools like Voyant Tools. Discover hidden insights by visualizing patterns, trends, and connections in their writings. Engage with interactive visualizations, word clouds, and frequency charts to uncover the thematic richness, linguistic features, and narrative styles of these remarkable women writers.

Maps

Explore our Maps section and discover the geographical landscapes linked to our authors. Delve into the places where these authors lived, worked, and drew inspiration for their works using interactive maps. Visualize the locations mentioned in their novels and the real-world settings that influenced them, gaining a deeper understanding of the connection between their creations and the physical spaces they inhabited.

Bibliography

Discover our meticulously curated annotated bibliography, where we have extensively researched and analyzed the works of women writers from early modernity. Our comprehensive collection of scholarly resources shines a light on their remarkable literary achievements, covering themes, narrative techniques, and cultural contexts. Each entry has been carefully evaluated for scholarly significance, providing a valuable reference tool for scholars, researchers, and enthusiasts.

OUR TEAM

Meet our team of dedicated researchers passionate about exploring Spanish women writers’ works. Our team includes experts in Spanish literature, gender studies, and cultural history who bring a wealth of knowledge and experience to our research efforts. We are committed to uncovering the unique voices and perspectives of Spanish women writers throughout history and to sharing their important contributions with the world. Get to know our team members below and discover their individual areas of expertise and research interests.

Elizabeth Lagresa-González

Director

Elizabeth S. Lagresa-González, Assistant Professor at the University of British Columbia, holds a Ph.D. in Romance Languages and Literatures from Harvard University. She specializes in early modern Spanish literature and culture, focusing on gender, cross-cultural, visual, and material studies. Her works include peer-reviewed articles, a critical edition and English translation of Bernat Metge’s Lo Somni / The Dream, and a forthcoming co-edited volume on “Literature and Economy in Spain and Beyond.” Her monograph, "The Business of Romance," explores the transculturation of objects and female subjects across national and disciplinary borders through the lens of new economic and material criticism.

María Fernanda Guzmán

Research Assitant

María Fernanda Guzmán was born in Orizaba, Veracruz, Mexico, and has loved to write since she was little. She studied literature at the Universidad de las Américas in Puebla and she has had two academic exchanges at City Beijing University (China) and the University of Salamanca (Spain). Recently she finished her M.A. in Hispanic Studies at the University of British Columbia. She has worked as an editor of books published by the Management of the Historic Center of Puebla. She has been also a high school teacher at Prepa Anahuac Puebla in her native Mexico. Her tastes are diverse: she loves streaming video games, reading historical novels and writing fantasy. Recently, she has published her first book Arcángel: los niños elegidos.

Jennifer Nagtegaal

Research Assistant

Jennifer Nagtegaal is a Ph.D. candidate in Hispanic Studies at UBC, specializing in animation and comics studies. Her research, funded by SSHRC, explores the intersection of these fields. Her forthcoming monograph, Politically Animated: Non-Fiction Animation from the Hispanic World, published by University of Toronto Press in August 2023, is the first comprehensive study of animated non-fiction from the Spanish-speaking world. As a Graduate Research Assistant, Jennifer contributed to the project by creating bibliographies for women writers such as Leonor de Meneses and Ana Francisca Abarca de Bolea, as well as annotating these bibliographies. She also translated the annotated bibliographies into English.

Mirella Reichenbach Livotti

Research Assistant

Mirella Reichenbach Livotti is a Master´s student in the Hispanic Studies Program at the University of British Columbia (UBC). She also completed her undergraduate studies with a Double Major in Sociology and Latin American Studies from the same university. Her research interest is centred primarily on the intersection of gender and race in contemporary Latin American literature. In addition, she is interested in the representations of gender-based violence and its connection to work being done by feminist social movements and intellectuals. For this project, Re-imagining the Hispanic Novella, Mirella is responsible for creating a critical annotated bibliography of the author María Egual and her novel El esclavo de su dama. .

Sarah Revilla

Research Assistant

Sarah Revilla is a Ph.D. student in Hispanic Studies at UBC. Her research lies at the intersection of gender and cultural studies, particularly interested in gendered violence, affect, and artivism (art+activism) in contemporary Mexican and Argentine fiction. Sarah contributed to this project by compiling and categorizing primary and secondary sources on four 17th-century Spanish female authors: María de Zayas y Sotomayor, Leonor de Meneses, Mariana de Carvajal y Saavedra, and Ana Francisca Abarca de Bolea y Mur. Another part of Sarah’s work included annotating Mariana de Carvajal y Saavedra’s Navidades de Madrid y noches entretenidas, identifying key themes and critical bibliography.

Patricio Robles

Research Assistant. Web Designer

Patricio Robles, a Ph.D. candidate in Hispanic Studies at UBC, focuses on texts about nature in the Southern Cone in his dissertation. Through the lenses of Affective theory and Ecocritical analysis, he explores the perception of nature and culture in post-Independence travel writing, gauchesca literature, and contemporary novels. With a background in Anthropology and Environmental Studies, Patricio is responsible for the website's design and the incorporation of external visualization tools. He also maintains connections with the digital humanities staff at UBC, ensuring up-to-date knowledge. Additionally, Patricio has contributed to the compilation of the annotated bibliography of Mariana de Carvajal y Saavedra.

Pamela Zamora

Research Assistant. Web Designer

Pamela Zamora is a doctoral candidate in Hispanic Studies at the University of British Columbia. Her dissertation revolves around how 20th and 21st Central American and Mexican fiction contests humanitarian crises such feminicido, indigenous genocide and forced migration in the neoliberal epoch. At the intersection of sound studies, feminist theory and migration studies, her work amines to trace the literary representation of those crises. Pamela collected primary and critical sources to develop an exhaustive bibliography database on female writers from the Golden Age..

VOYANT TOOLS

Voyant Tools is a web-based text reading and analysis environment. It is a scholarly project that is designed to facilitate reading and interpretive practices for digital humanities students and scholars as well as for the general public. Voyant Tools is part of the Hermeneutica project developed by Stéfan Sinclair and Geoffrey Rockwell.
